Based on this data analysis, there was a rapid growth in the Eco-Friendly car market. Overall, our data shows a rate of 45.34% year over year from 2012 to 2019. BEV type vehicles accounts for almost 55% of the Qty Sold, while Tesla as a Luxury class brand accounts for 25.08% over the whole period and 45% of the Qty sold in 2019.
therefore, we have 2 really important insights from this analysis. The first is the positive trend of the overall Eco-friendly car market. The second, however, is the fact that one luxury brand seems to be headed basically monopolize the US market with almost no competition. So where can we improve?

  • Improving Affordability 
Affordability is very important. While the majority of the vehicles on the roads are in the economy class, we need more affordable Eco-friendly vehicles so that the transition goes faster.

  • Improving Battery Capacity
Battery range for the BEV type vehicles seems to lag behind the range a full tank of fuel. Therefore, the research and development of a longer-range battery needs to be continued while still keeping in mind the importance of the affordability for a quick transition to a completely environment friendly automobile.

  • Improving EV Infrastructure
EV infrastructure is still way behind. While you can drive a regular vehicle from one coast to another with minimal time wasted refueling, it will take a lot of planning to drive a BEV type vehicle on the same route due to the lack of charging stations and the time it takes for an electric vehicle to get a full charge. 

  • Improving competition 
Encourage traditional car makers to join the Eco-friendly automobile segment will help create some level of competition. It will eventually lead to having more environment friendly vehicles on the road, dropping prices, and offering more choices to the customer.
